Designing MLFlow

An open source framework for the complete Machine Learning lifecycle

THE CHALLENGE

How to simplify machine learning development to democratize AI?

In the era of Artificial Intelligence, only a few companies are leveraging machine learning to improve the user experience. Why is that? As our CTO Matei Zaharia said in a blog post of introducing MLFlow:



Everyone who has tried to do machine learning development knows that it is complex.



How do we simplify machine learning development to democratize AI?

MY ROLE

As a researcher

I collaborate with my design manager and ML(machine learning) product managers to profile the ML persona and visualize the user journey.



As a designer

I lead the design on MLFlow project and work closely with product managers and engineers to make big data and AI simple.

ML Persona: Marie's story

Welcome to the real world

Marie is a new CS graduate. She took some courses in machine learning and has participated in a few Kaggle competitions. After school, she joined a medium size company as a data scientist. Soon she realized what she did before, was just a small part of the picture.

Why is that?

Why? What has changed since she practiced ML in school? One word: scale. By looking deeper, there are 3 key issues she needs to worry about in ML lifecycle. 

  • Big data: Data is the fuel. Accessing and preparing big volume and variety of raw data requires more computing resources and a new form of parallel processing.
  • Product quality: Business expects ML to drive innovation. One needs to try various ML algorithms and run multiple experiments (model training) to get the best result.
  • Production: After getting the best result (a model), next is to move it to production (deploy), it can be challenging due to a plethora of deployment tools and environment a model needs to run in. 

Ups and downs

Marie freaked out. Luckily she got a team to cover her back. She has a data engineer who helps process the data and another infrastructure engineer deploy the model. She can focus on what she specializes in - picking up the best model. 

So Marie went ahead and tried a bunch of different algorithms & hyper-parameters, she looked at the result, saw what's working, refined and tried again. It was a highly iterative process and soon she got lost in her hundreds of experiments. One of the experiments had the best result but what were the inputs? She could not reproduce the model. 



Introducing MLFlow

Matt, the data engineer in Marie's team sent her a link to MLFlow, a new open source machine learning platform from Apache Spark creator. Marie was not alone - hundreds of companies using ML have the same challenge: it is hard to track experiments and reproduce results.  

The MLFlow not only addresses one challenge. It also standardizes the way to package model and move it to production, which helped Marie downstream where she hands off her model to the infrastructure engineer. 

Is this our happy ending? Not yet, definitely a good start.

BEHIND THE SCENE



How does it feel like working on this project from a designer's perspective? Worthwhile. Here are 4 phases I went through.

PHASE 1

Do the homework and speak the same language

For a designer, this's probably the hardest part. The ML domain is complex and full of jargons. Besides reading ML tutorials and taking online courses, I also relied on our ML experts walking us through the ML workflow and the taxonomy so we can all speak the same language.

PHASE 2

Synthesize the learnings and visualize the journey

Next we conduct user interviews to further understand ML engineers' workflow and hear the painpoints in their day-to-day work. Patterns are emerging around model training & deployment & management. The feedback from the field also repeated similar challenges. We synthesize the learnings into a user journey map for easy reference and knowledge sharing.

PHASE 3

Design for MVP

For the first release, we addressed the model training challenge by providing a tracking UI to record and query experiments runs. To help user output a model that achieves high performance, we also provide a compare feature for run metrics visualization and comparison.

PHASE 4

Keep calm and carry on

After we released MLFlow at Spark Summit on June 5th 2018, we got great media coverage and lots of customer interests. We also saw positive community feedback. It is a big milestone but still a long way to go. We are working with our customers and the community to iterate and add more features. We also start to look at solving other challenges on model management and deployment. Stay tuned!

THE RESULT

It has been an amazing ride and I deeply appreciate the opportunity of working with and learning from the team. What I feel most proud of, is making AI simple for Marie and others.
